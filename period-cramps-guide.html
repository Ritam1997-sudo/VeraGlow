Period Cramps Relief: Causes, Exercises, Drinks & When to See a Doctor

February 19, 2026 8 min read Women’s Health

What You’ll Learn in This Guide

  • What period cramps (dysmenorrhea) are
  • Types of period cramps
  • How to relieve period cramps naturally
  • What not to do during cramps
  • Exercises and drinks that help
  • When to see a doctor

Period cramps are common and can range from mild discomfort to severe pain that interferes with daily life. Understanding what causes cramps and how to manage them properly can make a big difference in comfort and overall well-being.

Understanding Period Cramps (Dysmenorrhea)

Period cramps — medically called dysmenorrhea — affect up to 70–90% of menstruating women, especially teenagers and young adults.

Cramps usually begin 1–2 days before or at the start of menstruation and typically last 2–3 days. They occur because the uterus contracts to shed its lining, triggered by hormone-like substances called prostaglandins.

Types of Period Cramps

  • Primary Dysmenorrhea: Common menstrual cramps without any underlying medical condition.
  • Secondary Dysmenorrhea: Cramps caused by conditions like endometriosis, uterine fibroids, or pelvic inflammatory disease.

How to Relieve Period Cramps

1. Apply Heat

Heat relaxes uterine muscles and improves blood circulation.

  • Use a heating pad or hot water bottle for 15–20 minutes
  • Repeat as needed

2. Gentle Exercise

  • Walking
  • Light jogging
  • Swimming
  • Cycling
  • Yoga poses like Child’s Pose and Cat-Cow

3. Stay Hydrated

  • Drink 6–8 glasses of water daily
  • Ginger or chamomile tea may help reduce bloating

4. Eat Anti-Inflammatory Foods

  • Helpful: Leafy greens, salmon, nuts, bananas, dark chocolate (70%+)
  • Limit: Excess salt, sugar, processed foods, and caffeine

5. Use OTC Medication if Needed

NSAIDs like ibuprofen or naproxen reduce prostaglandins and work best when taken early.

What Not to Do During Period Cramps

  • Don’t ignore severe pain
  • Don’t stay completely inactive
  • Don’t skip meals
  • Don’t smoke
  • Don’t overconsume caffeine

Exercises for Period Cramps

Yoga & Stretching Poses

  • Child’s Pose: Relaxes back and pelvis
  • Cat-Cow: Stretches spine and eases lower back pain
  • Cobra Pose: Opens abdomen and reduces tension
  • Supine Twist: Relieves hip and back tightness
  • Butterfly Pose: Opens hips and eases pelvic discomfort

Light Cardio

  • Walking: Improves circulation
  • Gentle Jogging: Releases endorphins
  • Swimming: Low-impact muscle relaxation
  • Cycling: Boosts blood flow

Drinks That Help

  • Water: Reduces bloating
  • Ginger Tea: Natural anti-inflammatory
  • Chamomile Tea: Relaxes muscles
  • Warm Lemon Water: Supports digestion
  • Peppermint or Cinnamon Tea: Helps calm cramps

When to See a Doctor

  • Severe pain that disrupts daily life
  • Very heavy bleeding
  • Cramps lasting more than 3 days
  • Fever with pelvic pain
  • Pain that worsens over time

These symptoms could indicate conditions like endometriosis or uterine fibroids and should be evaluated by a healthcare professional.

Final Thoughts

Period cramps are common, but with the right care — heat therapy, gentle exercise, hydration, proper nutrition, and rest — they can be managed effectively. Always listen to your body and seek medical advice if symptoms are severe or persistent.
